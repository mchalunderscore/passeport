Replace an identity

If the recovery phrase or seed may be exposed, stop using the identity.

  1. Save and publish the old OpenPGP revocation certificate where appropriate.
  2. Record every service that uses an old public key.
  3. Create a replacement identity and verify its recovery phrase.
  4. Repair each integration and replace registered keys on remote services.
  5. Remove the old seed only after the replacement is recoverable.

Revoking OpenPGP does not revoke SSH, age, or Minisign keys. Replace those public keys separately wherever they were registered.
